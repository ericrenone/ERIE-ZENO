## Part I · The Quantum Zeno Effect as Expression Inversion

### I.1 The Standard Mechanism

In quantum mechanics, when a system is repeatedly measured to determine whether it remains in its initial state, the probability of finding it there approaches 1 as measurement frequency approaches infinity. The physical mechanism: each measurement collapses the wavefunction to an eigenstate of the measurement operator; between measurements, the system evolves away from this eigenstate into a superposition; at the next measurement, it collapses back. The probability of *not* collapsing back to the initial state is proportional to t² for small t — quadratic, not linear. Therefore, in the limit of infinitely frequent measurements, the system is frozen in its initial state.

The measurement basis is the key parameter: what the measurement operator resolves IS what the system gets frozen in. A system measured in basis {|0⟩, |1⟩} gets frozen at whichever state within that basis it initially occupies. A system measured in a different basis, or not measured at all, evolves freely.
